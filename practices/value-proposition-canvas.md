# Value Proposition Canvas

## What it is in Aim Praxis

The Value Proposition Canvas (VPC), developed by Osterwalder and collaborators, is treated in Aim Praxis as the second-outermost layer. It operates at the cycle time of weeks to months and is responsible for the fit between supplier and customer at the level of jobs, pains, and gains.

The standard VPC has two halves. On the customer side: jobs (what they're trying to get done), pains (what frustrates them), and gains (what they want beyond the job). On the supplier side: products and services, pain relievers, and gain creators. Fit is claimed when the supplier side addresses the customer side.

Aim Praxis reads this as a stock-and-flow system rather than a matching exercise.

## The systems reading

Customer-side elements are stocks with dynamics:

**Jobs** are continuous demands on the customer's attention and resources. Each job has a flow rate — energy, time, money spent trying to get it done.

**Pains** are stocks that fill when jobs go badly. Frustration accumulates, money wasted accumulates, time lost accumulates.

**Gains** are stocks that fill when jobs go well. Confidence, capability, status accumulate.

The customer is in constant flow. Jobs are running; pain and gain stocks are filling or draining. The actual pain and gain stocks are determined by the rates, not by static existence.

Supplier-side elements are valves:

**Products and services** carry the valves the supplier installs.

**Pain relievers** are valves that drain customer pain stocks faster than the customer could drain them alone.

**Gain creators** are valves that fill customer gain stocks faster than the customer could fill them alone.

## Fit, restated

Fit is not item matching between two lists. Fit is whether the supplier's valves are installed where the customer's stocks are most painfully accumulating or most below desired levels. The match is between rates, not lists.

This restatement explains why a value proposition can look perfect on paper and still fail. The supplier addresses pains the customer has, but the customer's pain stock isn't filling fast enough to make them feel the need. The supplier creates gains the customer would value, but the customer's existing gain stock is high enough that the marginal gain doesn't matter.

## Hidden customer stocks

The standard VPC omits several stocks that determine whether fit produces purchase:

**Switching cost stock.** Accumulated investment in the current solution. Balances against any new value proposition.

**Trust stock.** Customer's accumulated confidence that any supplier in the category can deliver. Low when customers have been burned; high in categories with strong reputation effects.

**Awareness stock.** Whether the customer knows the pain or gain exists. Subliminal pains require awareness-building before relief can produce response.

**Urgency stock.** The time derivative of the pain stock. Rising pain produces action; high but stable pain produces tolerance.

A perfect fit fails when any of these is below threshold.

## Three feedback loops

**Relief loop (reinforcing).** Pain drained → improved experience → more usage → deeper integration → higher switching costs in supplier's favor. Produces retention.

**Expectation loop (balancing).** Gain creators raise the gain stock → new baseline → previous gain creators no longer feel like gains. Produces habituation. The supplier must continuously add new gain creators.

**Disappointment loop (reinforcing in the wrong direction).** Promise fails → new pain emerges (frustration with the supplier) → larger than the original pain → customer becomes detractor. The most dangerous loop because it overrides retention.

## What the VPC catches

The VPC catches fit error — the product is heading toward the right segment but solving the wrong problem for them. Invisible to Story Mapping and below because those layers start with the assumption that fit is established.

## The diagnostic question

The most useful question the VPC enables: which of the customer's pain stocks is currently *rising* — not just high, but rising? Rising pain produces urgency. High but stable pain produces tolerance. The value proposition emphasis should align with which pain is in the rising phase.

## Cadence

Recommended cadence: quarterly review, or whenever significant customer signal accumulates. The customer research agent supports this layer by continuously monitoring customer signals for patterns that might invalidate fit assumptions.

## Integration with Story Mapping

The VPC's validated fit becomes the user journey that Story Mapping decomposes. The pains the team has committed to relieving and the gains the team has committed to creating define the user-facing work that Story Mapping organizes into deliverable structure.
